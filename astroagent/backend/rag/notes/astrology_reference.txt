PLANETS AND THEIR MEANINGS

The Sun
The Sun is the core of the natal chart. It represents your essential identity, ego, vitality, and life purpose. It shows what you need to feel alive and where you seek to shine. The Sun sign is the sign the Sun occupied at the moment of your birth, and it describes the broad strokes of your personality, the qualities you are developing in this lifetime.

The Moon
The Moon governs the emotional self, instincts, habits, memory, and the subconscious. It describes how you respond emotionally to life, what makes you feel safe and nurtured, and your relationship to the past and to your mother or primary caregiver. In a birth chart, the Moon sign shows your emotional nature and the sign it's in colors your inner life profoundly.

Mercury
Mercury rules the mind, communication, learning, and the way you think and process information. It governs how you speak, write, and make decisions. Mercury also rules short trips, siblings, and day-to-day exchanges. Its sign and house placement show how you learn and what kinds of thinking come naturally.

Venus
Venus is the planet of love, beauty, values, pleasure, and relating. It governs romantic attraction, aesthetics, financial values, and what you find desirable. In relationships, Venus describes what you offer and what you seek. Its placement shows your love language and what you find beautiful in the world.

Mars
Mars is the planet of action, desire, courage, and assertiveness. It governs how you pursue goals, handle anger and conflict, and assert yourself. Mars is raw energy and drive. Its sign shows your fighting style, your physical energy, and your approach to getting what you want.

Jupiter
Jupiter is the great benefic — the planet of expansion, wisdom, abundance, and growth. It shows where luck and opportunity flow, where you are generous and optimistic, and what philosophies or beliefs sustain you. Jupiter's house placement shows the area of life most blessed with growth.

Saturn
Saturn is the planet of discipline, responsibility, structure, and karma. It governs life lessons, long-term commitments, and the maturation process. Saturn shows where you face your greatest challenges and where, through effort and persistence, you can achieve lasting mastery. Saturn's lessons are hard but ultimately rewarding.

Uranus
Uranus governs innovation, rebellion, sudden change, and liberation. It disrupts the status quo and brings radical transformation. Generationally, Uranus shows where society breaks new ground. In personal charts, its house shows where you need freedom and originality.

Neptune
Neptune rules dreams, spirituality, illusion, compassion, and the dissolution of boundaries. It governs the mystical, the artistic, the idealistic. Neptune can dissolve structures, confuse, or inspire transcendence. Its house shows where you seek the divine or risk being deceived.

Pluto
Pluto is the planet of transformation, power, death, and rebirth. It governs deep psychological processes, shared resources, and the underworld of the psyche. Pluto's house shows where you experience profound transformation and where power dynamics play out in your life.

THE TWELVE ZODIAC SIGNS

Aries (March 21 – April 19) — Cardinal Fire
Bold, pioneering, impulsive, courageous, and self-starting. Aries is the first sign, the initiator of the zodiac. Aries energy is direct, honest, and sometimes blunt. The shadow side includes impatience, aggression, and self-centeredness. Aries is ruled by Mars.

Taurus (April 20 – May 20) — Fixed Earth
Steadfast, sensual, patient, reliable, and stubborn. Taurus values stability, beauty, and material comfort. Taurus energy is grounding, nurturing, and persistent. Shadow: possessiveness and resistance to change. Ruled by Venus.

Gemini (May 21 – June 20) — Mutable Air
Curious, adaptable, communicative, dual-natured, and witty. Gemini thrives on variety, information, and social exchange. Shadow: inconsistency and anxiety. Ruled by Mercury.

Cancer (June 21 – July 22) — Cardinal Water
Nurturing, intuitive, emotional, protective, and home-loving. Cancer feels everything deeply and prizes family and security. Shadow: moodiness and clinging. Ruled by the Moon.

Leo (July 23 – August 22) — Fixed Fire
Generous, dramatic, confident, creative, and warm-hearted. Leo seeks recognition and leads with heart and flair. Shadow: vanity and need for approval. Ruled by the Sun.

Virgo (August 23 – September 22) — Mutable Earth
Analytical, service-oriented, precise, health-conscious, and discerning. Virgo refines and improves. Shadow: over-criticism and anxiety. Ruled by Mercury.

Libra (September 23 – October 22) — Cardinal Air
Harmonious, fair-minded, relationship-focused, diplomatic, and aesthetically refined. Libra seeks balance and beauty. Shadow: indecisiveness and people-pleasing. Ruled by Venus.

Scorpio (October 23 – November 21) — Fixed Water
Intense, transformative, secretive, powerful, and deeply perceptive. Scorpio probes beneath the surface and is unafraid of darkness. Shadow: jealousy and manipulation. Ruled by Pluto (and Mars).

Sagittarius (November 22 – December 21) — Mutable Fire
Adventurous, philosophical, optimistic, freedom-loving, and direct. Sagittarius seeks truth and wisdom through experience. Shadow: restlessness and tactlessness. Ruled by Jupiter.

Capricorn (December 22 – January 19) — Cardinal Earth
Ambitious, disciplined, pragmatic, patient, and reserved. Capricorn builds toward lasting achievement with quiet determination. Shadow: rigidity and workaholism. Ruled by Saturn.

Aquarius (January 20 – February 18) — Fixed Air
Innovative, humanitarian, unconventional, intellectual, and future-oriented. Aquarius envisions progress and community. Shadow: detachment and contrarianism. Ruled by Uranus (and Saturn).

Pisces (February 19 – March 20) — Mutable Water
Compassionate, dreamy, intuitive, boundaryless, and spiritually oriented. Pisces connects to the universal and the transcendent. Shadow: escapism and victimhood. Ruled by Neptune (and Jupiter).

THE TWELVE HOUSES

First House (House of Self)
The Ascendant and first house describe your outer personality, physical appearance, and the mask you wear in the world. It shows how others perceive you at first meeting and how you initiate action.

Second House (House of Value)
Rules personal resources, money, possessions, and self-worth. It shows how you earn, spend, and relate to material security.

Third House (House of Communication)
Governs communication, learning, siblings, short travel, and the local environment. It describes your thinking style and how you express yourself day-to-day.

Fourth House (House of Home)
The IC and fourth house describe home, family, roots, and emotional foundations. It shows your relationship to the past, your parents (especially the mother), and your private inner world.

Fifth House (House of Creativity)
Rules self-expression, creativity, romance, children, play, and pleasure. It describes how you create and what brings you joy.

Sixth House (House of Service and Health)
Governs daily routines, health habits, work (daily tasks), and service. It shows how you attend to your body and how you contribute through daily work.

Seventh House (House of Partnership)
The Descendant and seventh house rule committed partnerships — romantic and business. It shows what you seek in others and how you relate in close one-on-one bonds.

Eighth House (House of Transformation)
Rules shared resources, intimacy, death, rebirth, inheritance, and deep psychological transformation. It shows where power and depth enter your life.

Ninth House (House of Philosophy)
Governs higher education, long-distance travel, philosophy, religion, and the search for meaning. It shows your worldview and where you expand your horizons.

Tenth House (House of Career)
The Midheaven and tenth house describe career, public reputation, authority, and life direction. It shows how you are seen in the world and what you are here to achieve publicly.

Eleventh House (House of Community)
Rules friendships, groups, social causes, hopes, and dreams. It shows your relationship to community and collective ideals.

Twelfth House (House of the Hidden)
Governs the unconscious, hidden enemies, solitude, spiritual retreat, and karma. It describes what lies beneath the surface and what must be healed or released.

ASPECTS

Conjunction (0°, orb 8°): Planets merge their energies. Can be harmonious or intense depending on the planets involved.

Sextile (60°, orb 6°): A flowing, opportunistic aspect. Natural talent and ease, but requires some effort to activate.

Square (90°, orb 8°): Tension and challenge. The planets involved are at cross-purposes, creating friction that demands resolution and growth.

Trine (120°, orb 8°): Harmony and natural flow. The planets work together effortlessly, though sometimes without enough tension to motivate growth.

Opposition (180°, orb 8°): Polarization between two areas of life. Calls for integration and balance between opposing energies.
